ASocks IPv6 proxies overview

IPv6 proxies use the newer 128-bit IPv6 address space instead of the exhausted IPv4 pool, giving providers access to an almost limitless supply of IPs at very low cost. This makes them ideal for high-volume tasks against targets that fully support IPv6, where you can rotate through enormous subnets cheaply. The catch is that many sites are still IPv4-only, so IPv6 proxies only work where the destination speaks IPv6.

ASocks positions itself as “Budget residential SOCKS5 pool with flexible targeting”, with several million residential IPs and per GB pricing from ~$3/GB (residential).
