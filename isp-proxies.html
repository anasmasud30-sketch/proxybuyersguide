Quick answer

ISP proxies, also called static residential proxies, are IP addresses registered under a consumer internet service provider but hosted on fast servers in a data center. This hybrid gives you the residential trust score that targets respect combined with the raw speed and stability of a datacenter connection. Because they are static and dedicated, they hold a single trusted IP for as long as you need it.

How ISP proxies work

The provider acquires IP blocks that are registered to residential ISPs at the ASN level, then hosts them on high-bandwidth servers. Targets performing a lookup see a legitimate residential ISP owner, while you get near-datacenter latency and 24/7 uptime. Since each IP is dedicated and unchanging, it is perfect for long-lived logged-in sessions.

Without proxies, every request leaves the same IP fingerprint, so rate limits and bans arrive fast.

Pools are modest in size, built from ISP-registered IP blocks hosted on servers and assigned as dedicated static addresses.

Advantages and trade-offs

Advantages

  • Residential-level trust with datacenter-level speed
  • Static, dedicated IPs stay stable for weeks or months
  • No per-gigabyte metering, so heavy usage stays predictable
  • Consistent uptime unlike device-dependent residential pools
  • Ideal for warming and maintaining persistent accounts

Trade-offs

  • Smaller, less diverse pools than rotating residential networks
  • Higher per-IP cost than plain datacenter proxies
  • A burned static IP must be replaced rather than rotated away
  • Limited city-level geo options compared with residential

Frequently Asked Questions

Are ISP proxies good for long-term account management and warming?

Yes — ISP proxies suit long-term account management and warming, E-commerce and marketplace selling, and streaming and geo-locked media access, because they pair datacenter speed with ISP-registered static IPs.

What is the detection risk with ISP proxies?

Detection risk is rated low — because the IPs are registered to consumer ISPs yet hosted on fast infrastructure, they blend in while staying quick.

What should I look for when buying ISP proxies?

Prioritise transparent per-unit pricing, pool size and freshness, geo-targeting granularity, and a small trial so you can benchmark success rate before committing.

How much do ISP proxies cost?

ISP proxies are typically priced per IP at around $2-$7/IP per month. Higher volumes lower the effective rate, so estimate your monthly usage before choosing a plan.
