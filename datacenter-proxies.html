Quick answer

Datacenter proxies are IP addresses hosted on servers in commercial data centers rather than tied to a home internet subscription. They are the fastest and most affordable proxy type, making them ideal for bandwidth-heavy scraping, SEO monitoring, and any task where raw throughput matters more than blending in. The tradeoff is that their IP ranges are easy for sophisticated sites to identify and block.

How datacenter proxies work

Your traffic passes through IPs owned by hosting providers and cloud platforms, delivered as either shared or dedicated addresses. Because these IPs sit on server subnets with predictable ASN ownership, they route with very low latency but also carry a recognizable non-residential fingerprint. Rotation and subnet diversity are used to spread requests and avoid rate limits.

A single origin IP is trivially easy for modern anti-bot systems to throttle or blacklist.

Pools are built from server subnets across major hosting providers and are far smaller and less diverse than residential networks.

Advantages and trade-offs

Advantages

  • Fastest speeds and lowest latency of any proxy type
  • Cheapest cost per IP, often billed as flat monthly rate
  • Effectively unlimited bandwidth on most plans
  • Dedicated options give you exclusive, predictable IPs
  • Great for high-concurrency, non-sensitive scraping at scale

Trade-offs

  • Easily detected and blocked by anti-bot systems and major platforms
  • Shared IPs can arrive pre-flagged from other users' abuse
  • Limited geo-diversity compared with residential pools
  • Not suitable for sneaker sites, social media, or strict targets

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do datacenter proxies cost?

Datacenter proxies are typically priced per IP at around $0.50-$2/IP per month. Higher volumes lower the effective rate, so estimate your monthly usage before choosing a plan.

What is the detection risk with datacenter proxies?

Detection risk is rated high — because the IPs belong to known hosting providers, sophisticated targets can identify and block them more readily.

How fast are datacenter proxies?

Speed is very high compared with other proxy types. Your traffic passes through IPs owned by hosting providers and cloud platforms, delivered as either shared or dedicated addresses.

Are datacenter proxies good for high-volume scraping of unprotected sites?

Yes — datacenter proxies suit high-volume scraping of unprotected sites, SEO rank tracking and keyword research, and price and market intelligence gathering, because they come from fast commercial server IP ranges.

What should I look for when buying datacenter proxies?

Prioritise transparent per-unit pricing, pool size and freshness, geo-targeting granularity, and a small trial so you can benchmark success rate before committing.
